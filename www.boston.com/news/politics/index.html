‘People’s Pledge’ may end up boxing in Scott Brown for campaign against Elizabeth Warren

When Scott Brown maneuvered Elizabeth Warren earlier this year into signing an agreement banning third-party advertising in their highly anticipated US Senate race, it was a public relations coup for the Republican incumbent. But without a third-party to do that dirty work for him, though, Brown may be forced to do it himself. And doing so could risk his key strategic advantage - his likability and the everyman persona crafted with his pickup truck, barncoat, and basketball playing.

World more dangerous, top general tells Harvard

Army General Martin E. Dempsey, the chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, tells students at a forum at Harvard’s Kennedy School that even though the world appears to enjoy greater stability and interdependence, threats looming beneath the surface -- from cyber warfare to the proliferation of long-range missiles -- actually place American security at greater risk. “That’s the essence of what I like to call the security paradox.”

Navy urged to name ship for living Bay State veteran

Thomas Hudner, 87, of Concord earned the nation’s highest award for valor after he purposely crash-landed his plane in the Korean War to try to save the Navy’s first African-American pilot. Now the Navy is being urged by a growing chorus including the Bay State’s two US senators to name a new warship after him, a rare honor for a living person more commonly bestowed only on American presidents.

Despite a new ad praising Fenway, Scott Brown pushed in 2001 to move the Red Sox to Foxborough

In a new radio ad released today, US Senator Scott Brown wraps himself around the legendary Fenway Park. What Brown doesn’t mention in the spot is that it represents 180-degree turn from the public position he took nearly a decade ago. Back in 2001, when the Red Sox ownership was looking to relocate the Red Sox, Brown, then a state representative, not only pushed to remove the team from the storied park, but also out of Boston.

Michelle Obama tweets support for mothers after Democratic strategist criticizes Ann Romney

First lady Michelle Obama today expressed her support for mothers of all stripes, after a Democratic strategist rankled Republicans by suggesting Ann Romney “has actually never worked a day in her life” because the wife of presumptive GOP nominee Mitt Romney had been a stay-at-home mom. “Every mother works hard, and every woman deserves to be respected,” she said. The debate was part substantive and part theatrics, as the rival campaigns settled into their new roles.
