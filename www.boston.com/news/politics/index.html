Scott Brown campaigns with Rudy Giuliani in Boston’s North End, while Elizabeth Warren visits Lowell gym

US Senator Scott Brown campaigned in Boston’s North End Friday with former New York City mayor and fellow Republican Rudy Giuliani. Their visit drew a large crowd of supporters, who accompanied Brown down Hanover Street, chanting “Go, Scott, go!” Later Friday, Senate challenger Elizabeth Warren delivered her stump speech from inside a boxing ring at Ramalho’s West End Gym in Lowell, to a large and enthusiastic crowd. A Warren campaign poster was tacked to a wall beneath a sign for boxers that read: “No Pain, No Gain.”

Mitt Romney vows to be an agent of real change

In a raucous, thunderous rally, Mitt Romney pilloried President Obama Friday night for not being more uplifting – either in his rhetoric or with the economy – and vowed to be a genuine agent of change. As the campaign for president narrowed to a few critical hours, Romney and Republicans held a mini-convention of sorts in the conservative southwest of the pivotal state of Ohio. The event served as an pep rally lauding the nominee and launching dozens of high-profile surrogates across Ohio and into other swing states in the final days of campaigning.

President Obama decries Mitt Romney’s ad on the auto bailout

President Obama, making last-minute appeals to voters in Ohio, ridiculed Mitt Romney and said his Republican rival was misleading voters over recent ads about the auto industry bailout. Obama, commenting directly for the first time on ads run in Ohio and Michigan suggesting that Chrysler will move jobs to China, said Romney was trying to “scare hard working Americans to scare up votes.” The battle lines in Ohio, the most crucial of the battleground states, have formed around the auto industry and the federal bailout backed by Obama and largely opposed by Romney.

Some Facebook users ‘dislike’ being used in online advertising for Mitt Romney

Mitt Romney’s Facebook page records 11.6 million “likes,” but not everybody on his page are true fans. Indeed some are wondering how their names are popping up on the online social network as an apparent supporter of the Republican presidential candidate.

Barack Obama campaign fights Mitt

The Barack Obama campaign will deliver a counterpunch to Mitt Romney’s misleading auto ads with spots of its own in Michigan and Ohio that attempt to set the record straight and raise questions about Romney’s character. The Obama ad splices together news headlines and stories calling Romney’s ads “wholly inaccurate” and “clearly misleading.”
