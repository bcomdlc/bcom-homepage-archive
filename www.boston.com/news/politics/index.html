Scott Brown treads carefully with friend Mayor Menino when asked during chat about Chick-fil-A

Senator Scott Brown, who has earned kind words from Boston Mayor Thomas M. Menino despite their differing political parties, today tread carefully when asked during a Boston.com chat about the city leader’s spat with Chick-fil-A over its opposition to gay marriage. Menino has pointedly withheld his endorsement of fellow Democrat Elizabeth Warren as she challenges Brown, a Republican, for reelection. Brown has cultivated support from the mayor, and played up the bipartisan theme at several points during the 45-minute chat.

Elizabeth Warren, in new TV ad, calls for more infrastructure investments

Voter registration drive in Israel tries to get Americans there to vote in the United States

There are some 250,000 American citizens living in Israel, many of whom are eligible to vote in the US elections. Organizers for a voter-registration drive in Israel are trying to make the point that they are a vital constituency. It’s unlikely to make a significant difference in the presidential race, but because voters register in their last place of US residence, it could swing some congressional races.
