Scott Brown launches new TV ad, again focused on bipartisanship

In a new television ad, Senator Scott Brown again emphasizes the theme of bipartisanship, while continuing his push for women voters. The spot features Brown speaking with an unemployed single mother living in Shrewsbury. Brown says: “People like Diane keep me focused on what really matters. To me, creating jobs is more important than what party you belong to. That’s why one of the first votes I took as a senator was for a Democratic jobs bill.”

Mitt Romney paid 14.1 percent in federal taxes in 2011

Mitt Romney filed his 2011 tax returns today and released a 20-year snapshot of his tax returns -- capping a difficult week by returning to the campaign a topic that has dogged the candidacy of the Republican presidential nominee since the start of the primaries. Romney’s updated tax returns, which will be posted online in full at 3 p.m. on Friday, show that he paid $1.9 million in taxes on $13.7 million in income, most of which came from his investments. It means his effective tax rate for 2011 was 14.1 percent.

Before acrimony, Mitt Romney sought prose from Peggy Noonan’s now-poison pen

More than a decade ago, long before Peggy Noonan would emerge as one of the most prominent conservative critics of Mitt Romney’s presidential campaign, the two sought to make history together. Romney, during the run-up to the 2002 Olympic Games, wanted someone to help draft remarks for the opening ceremonies. Noonan, a well-known speechwriter for President Reagan, was a logical choice. So she came up with a draft speech. Romney decided to scrap it.
