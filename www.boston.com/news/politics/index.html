Live-blogging the election

The Boston Globe will be posting updates here all night as results roll in and the candidates celebrate and concede. 



No early surprises as President Obama, Mitt Romney pick up expected victories

The early polls have closed, and Mitt Romney holds an early advantage over President Obama, 76 electoral votes to 64. Romney has picked up early victories in Kentucky, West Virginia, Indiana, South Carolina, Oklahoma, Tennessee, Georgia and Alabama. Obama has won Vermont, Illinois, Connecticut, Maine, Delaware, Rhode Island, Maryland, Massachusetts and the District of Columbia.   




The president has Election Night dinner with family after basketball tradition

President Obama had dinner on Election Night with his wife, Michelle, and daughters Malia and Sasha, who flew after school from Washington with their maternal grandmother, Marian Robinson.



Turnout in New Hampshire high

Turnout in New Hampshire is healthy, at least by the measure of lines. David Scanlan, deputy secretary of state, said lines were unwieldy at some polling places in Manchester, Raymond, and Salem. State officials, including some from the attorney general’s office, were dispatched to help local poll watchers manage the crowds. 



Gay marriage vote dominates election day in Maine

PORTLAND, Maine — Throughout the downtown streets of Maine’s largest city, lawn signs, bumper stickers, and door hangers were abundant. But most weren’t pushing for Barack Obama or Mitt Romney. Instead, Question 1 -- a vote on gay-marriage -- appeared to be the most contested issue.  Three years ago, the same initiative failed here: 53 percent of residents voted to nullify a piece of legislation that would have allowed gays and lesbians to legally wed.
