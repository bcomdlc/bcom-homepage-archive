Tax returns show Scott Brown, Elizabeth Warren among nation’s top income group

The Massachusetts US Senate race, already framed in terms of electing the best guardian for the middle class, will be fought by two candidates in the nation’s top income group. Tax returns show Scott Brown’s income more than doubled to nearly $840,000 after he was elected in 2010, while Elizabeth Warren had nearly $1 million in earnings two of the past four years. Last year, she was in the top 1 percent while he was in the top 2 percent.

Governor Deval Patrick to address Iowa Democrats this summer, raising questions about his presidential plans for 2016

Governor Deval Patrick will deliver the keynote address to an Iowa Democratic Party dinner in June, in the latest sign that he may be considering a run for president in 2016. Patrick will speak to Democrats in the first-in-the-nation caucus state on June 15 at the Community Choice Credit Union Convention Center in Des Moines, an aide said today. The visit will be his first introduction to key party activists in a state that plays a pivotal role in presidential elections.

Democrats say tax returns will counter Scott Brown’s regular-guy image

Democrats are attempting to spin today’s release of tax returns in the Scott Brown-Elizabeth Warren US Senate race, arguing that Brown’s returns will show he, too, is a wealthy individual despite the regular-guy image the Republican cultivates. “When Scott Brown went to Washington, Massachusetts families barely knew him,” Massachusetts Democratic Party Chairman John Walsh said. “Now, it’s clear there’s a millionaire under that $675 barn jacket, with as much as $100,000 in big oil and big bank stocks.”

Rep. Ed Markey argues cyber security bill would be ineffective and violate consumer privacy

US Representative Edward J. Markey on Thursday called on fellow House members to reject a bill designed to bolster cyber security, saying it would fail to accomplish its goal and would compromise consumer privacy. In a statement ahead of House deliberations, the Obama administration raised similar concerns and said the president would veto the bill, if it reached his desk. The House plans to vote Friday on the Cyber Intelligence Sharing and Protection Act.

Report: Former anti-debt candidate Bill Hudak files for personal bankruptcy

The Boston Business Journal reports that former congressional candidate Bill Hudak — “a fiscal conservative whose campaign website includes a national debt ticker” — filed for personal bankruptcy protection today. In his bankruptcy filing, Hudak listed assets of $1.06 million and liabilities of $897,000, the BBJ said. Hudak, a Boxford resident and conservative Republican, ran a flame-throwing campaign against US Representative John Tierney in 2010. Hudak had been planning another run before bowing out of the race in January.
