Mitt Romney stops negative advertising as Rick Santorum tends to ill daughter

Mitt Romney is backing off a plan to aggressively target Rick Santorum in his home state, as Santorum takes a break from the campaign trail to tend to his ill daughter. The Romney campaign on Monday asked Pennsylvania television stations to pull a negative ad against Santorum and replace it with a positive one about the former Massachusetts governor. Romney had planned a deluge of ads designed to wrest votes from Santorum ahead of the state’s April 24 primary.

Vice President Joe Biden to promote “Buffett Rule” in New Hampshire Thursday

Vice President Joe Biden will make a campaign stop in Exeter, N.H., Thursday to advocate for the president’s proposal that the wealthiest Americans pay at least 30 percent of their income in taxes. Biden will deliver his remarks at the Exeter Town Hall just days ahead of a Senate vote on the so-called Buffett Rule, named after billionaire investor Warren Buffett who has said he should not be paying a lower tax rate than his secretary.

Elizabeth Warren raises $6.9 million in first quarter, more than double Senator Scott Brown

US Senate candidate Elizabeth Warren’s campaign said today that she raised $6.9 million in the first three months of the year, continuing the Democratic challenger’s staggering pace and doubling the figure collected by incumbent Scott Brown. Brown, a Republican, said through his campaign on Friday he raised $3.4 million in the first three months of the year, giving the Republican a total of about $15 million in the bank.

Religious leaders say on Easter that faith and politics are inseparable

Influential pastor Rick Warren defended the place of religion in politics during a television interview that aired Sunday morning, arguing that “faith is simply a world view.” Warren made his statements only a few weeks after a Pew Research Center survey showed 38 percent of Americans -- an all-time high -- believe political leaders spend too much time talking about religion. A majority, 54 percent, said churches should stay out of political debates. The role of religion in politics has been a subject of debate during the presidential election. The three leading Republican candidates have described a “war on religion” in America.

John Kerry becomes grandfather for first time

Senator John Kerry is a grandfather for the first time. His younger daughter, Vanessa, delivered a baby boy on Easter Sunday. Alexander Kerry Nahed arrived at Massachusetts General Hospital weighing 7 pounds, 11 ounces, and measuring 20 1/2 inches. Both of his parents are doctors at the hospital. Kerry has been step-grandfather to children born to the children of his wife, Teresa Heinz Kerry, but Alexander Nahed is the first child of his own daughters, Vanessa and Alexandra Kerry.
