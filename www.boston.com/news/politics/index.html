Stanley Cup shines for a day on Massachusetts congressional delegation

In an environment fraught with political peril, where a single off-the-cuff remark or ill-advised tweet can extinguish a career, there are few bets safer than posing for photographs with a championship trophy won by the hometown team.

State GOP releases attack ad mocking Elizabeth Warren and her Hollywood fundraising trip

The state Republican Party, which has been ridiculing Elizabeth Warren for fundraising in Hollywood, released a cutting video today that mocks the Democratic US Senate candidate for being a Hollywood elitist. The web-only video is in black and white, and edited to look like an old-fashioned newsreel or movie trailer. Warren is attending a Hollywood fundraiser Thursday hosted by liberal producer Norman Lear and entertainer Barbara Streisand.

Ohio looms as most important of Super Tuesday states in race for the GOP nomination

Mitt Romney, fresh from wins last night in Michigan and Arizona, immediately traveled here to the next major battleground state to try to eat into a lead currently held by Rick Santorum.

Rick Santorum regrets ‘throw-up’ remark about John Kennedy stance on church-state divide

Former Pennsylvania Senator Rick Santorum has said he regrets his statement that he “almost threw up” after reading John F. Kennedy’s famous speech on the separation of church and state.

Rick Santorum leads Mitt Romney in poll in Ohio, a Super Tuesday state

Former Pennsylvania Senator Rick Santorum is leading former Massachusetts Governor Mitt Romney in the crucial Super Tuesday state of Ohio by 11 points, according to the Ohio Poll, a new poll conducted by the Institute for Policy Research at the University of Cincinnati. The poll – which was conducted before Romney’s wins yesterday in Michigan and Arizona – found Santorum with 37 percent support from likely Republican primary voters, compared to 26 percent for Romney.
