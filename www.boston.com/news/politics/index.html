Senate passes bill to repeal medical device tax; industry urges House to do the same

Medical-device companies scored a political victory when the Senate voted in a non-binding resolution to repeal a new device tax, and now they are turning their attention to the House, especially U.S. Representative Ed Markey. The 2.3 percent tax went into effect in January and is supposed to help offset the costs of implementing President Obama’s landmark health reform law. But the device industry argues that it would cost Massachusetts’ largest companies more than $411 million a year, according to a new analysis by the Pioneer Institute that will be released in April, just before the first payment is due.

Gabriel Gomez raises $350,000 since Feb. 12 in Republican Senate primary race

Republican candidate for US Senate Gabriel Gomez has collected more than $350,000 since Feb. 12, his campaign said Thursday morning. The campaign said that over 70 percent of the contributions were under $100, a small-donation metric that campaigns frequently cite to boast of grassroots support.

City Councilor John R. Connolly launches bid for Boston mayor at downtown rally

City Councilor John R. Connolly packed a hotel ballroom Wednesday night for the kickoff of his bid for Boston mayor. Patrick trips online firestorm with re-election joke

For a handful of minutes early Wednesday afternoon, the Massachusetts political world was in flames. Governor Deval Patrick’s attempt at humor during an appearance at the University of Massachusetts at Boston went viral, leading many to believe that he would seek a third term. That would have been newsy, indeed, since Patrick has long professed to be satisfied with two terms. And, while there is no law prohibiting a third consecutive term, there is no modern precedent.

Elizabeth Warren shopping a book proposal on her experiences fighting Wall Street in Washington

Senator Elizabeth Warren is planning to write a new book, one that the Massachusetts Democrat is pitching to publishers as a first-hand account of her battle for the middle class, delving into both her proposal for the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau and her race for the US Senate. The book’s working title is “Rigged,” and she is being represented by well-known Washington agent Robert Barnett, who also negotiated the book deal for Scott Brown, the Republican predecessor who Warren defeated in November’s election.
