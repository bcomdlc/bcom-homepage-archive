Counterterrorism chief: no active plot by Al Qaeda on 1-year anniversary of Osama bin Laden’s death

The president’s chief counterterrorism adviser said Sunday there is no indication Al Qaeda is planning a terrorist attack on the one-year anniversary of the killing of Osama bin Laden. John Brennan argued Al Qaeda’s capability “has been degraded significantly” since the death of its leader. Still, Brennan cautioned that “we can’t rest,” and said the Department of Homeland Security is particularly concerned about al Qaeda’s presence in Yemen.

Hilary Rosen repeats apology to Ann Romney but won’t concede she is qualified to speak on women’s economic concerns

Two weeks after she backed out of a television appearance, Democratic strategist Hilary Rosen returned to the air Sunday to address her assertion that Ann Romney is unqualified to speak for women’s economic concerns. Rosen ducked “Meet the Press” host David Gregory’s question about whether Ann Romney can talk knowledgeably about the economic issues facing American women. “I’ll leave that to Mitt Romney to decide who he talks to,” Rosen said. “I’m not going to go there.”

Campaigns exchange barbs after President Obama’s ad questions whether Mitt Romney would have ordered raid on Osama bin Laden

The Obama and Romney campaigns traded jabs Sunday over the president's new TV ad questioning whether his Republican challenger would have ordered the raid that killed Al Qaeda leader Osama bin Laden a year ago this week. Romney adviser Ed Gillespie said Obama was exploiting the raid as a “divisive, partisan, political attack.” Obama adviser Robert Gibbs countered that “There’s a difference in the roles they would play as commander-in-chief, and I think that’s fair game.”

Scott Brown sinks half-court shot in front of excited Cape Cod kids

He was known as “Downtown Scottie Brown” for his jump-shooting ability in college, but Senator Scott Brown has proven he’s also “Next-Zip Code Scottie Brown.” The Massachusetts Republican sunk an underhand, half-court basketball shot on Friday while visiting the Hyannis Youth and Community Center. Brown then ran off the court, arms raised in victory, as children at the center cheered him on. The scene was captured on video by Brown’s team and distributed by email.

Scott Brown’s campaign calls on Elizabeth Warren to apologize for allowing Harvard to tout her as Native American; Warren refuses

US Senator Scott Brown’s campaign called on Democratic challenger Elizabeth Warren Friday to apologize for letting Harvard Law School tout her as a Native American in its attempts to demonstrate faculty diversity in the 1990s. But Warren said she didn’t know Harvard was promoting her that way -- until Friday. The Boston Herald reported Friday that Harvard had touted Warren’s Native American background as the school faced criticism for a faculty dominated by white men. Warren’s maternal grandparents both had Native American lineage.
