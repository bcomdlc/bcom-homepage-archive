Mitt Romney’s view of China changes after welcoming its Olympic bid in 2001

The first television ads of Mitt Romney’s general election campaign boasted of how he would confront China’s leaders, whom he branded “cheaters” on economic policy. He would be a president, he said, who “stands up to China on trade and demands they play by the rules.” Yet before he held public office — at a crucial moment for the communist foreign government — he was one of the chief Western voices arguing that critics should overlook China’s human rights record and welcome their application for a top ticket to international legitimacy: hosting the Olympic Games in Beijing.
