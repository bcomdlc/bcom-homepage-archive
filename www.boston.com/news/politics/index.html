After six years of campaigning, Mitt Romney faces specter of presidency - or private life

Mitt Romney wrapped up his pre-Election Day quest for the presidency with a rally Monday in downtown Manchester, N.H. The promise of the White House was as tantalizingly close as the bitterness of defeat. Hanging in the balance was his election as the nation’s 45th president, or the prospect of seeing his Secret Service detail pack up Wednesday and depart. That he was in that position, though, is testament to Romney’s development and endurance as a national political figure.

Mitt Romney says final rally in N.H. ‘special moment’ for presidential campaign

Mitt Romney returned to New Hampshire Monday night for his final campaign rally before the polls opened in the 2012 general election. The former Massachusetts governor and his wife, Ann, were overwhelmed with emotion as they received a minutes-long standing ovation from a crowd of thousands gathered in Manchester. “This is a special moment for Ann and me, because this is where our campaign began,” Romney said, his voice hoarse after speaking in three other states earlier in the day.

Chris Christie moved to tears by hug from Bruce Springsteen

New Jersey Governor Chris Christie said on Monday that he was moved to tears by recent interactions with singer Bruce Springsteen, who hugged the governor at a benefit concert for victims of Hurricane Sandy on Friday and spoke with Christie by phone on Monday while flying aboard Air Force One with President Obama. “Bruce said to me how proud he was of his state and how proud he was of the people of this state,” Christie said.

Electoral College provides many paths to 270

Get out your calculator. In one sense, Electoral College math is simple: Add up states’ electoral votes until one presidential candidate’s total is 270 or greater. In another sense, it is complex because there are many ways for each candidate to achieve that sum. Based on polling data aggregated by the website RealClearPolitics, President Obama has 201 electoral votes safely in the bank. Republican challenger Mitt Romney has 191. The remaining 146 electoral votes are spread among 11 battleground states.

Scott Brown, Elizabeth Warren end final day of campaigning with major rallies

Senator Scott Brown and Elizabeth Warren raced around the state today from early morning until evening, hugging babies and rallying supporters as they made one final push for votes on the last day of campaigning in the tight race for US Senate. Riding a campaign bus, Brown made nine stops that took him from Lynn to Worcester, to his hometown of Wrentham for one last evening rally. Warren ended the night with an outdoor rally before hundreds of supporters, many of them union members.
