Mitt Romney files for extension on 2011 taxes

Mitt Romney has filed for an extension on his 2011 taxes, his campaign said Friday. The Republican candidate for president and former Massachusetts governor estimated his 2011 tax liability at $3.2 million, according to the extension filing. He reported paying more than that, $3.4 million. Romney plans to file his return sometime in the next six months, and prior to the election will release it, according to a spokeswoman, Andrea Saul.

President Obama paid a federal tax rate of 20.5 percent in 2011, according to returns

President Obama and the first lady paid $162,074 in taxes in 2011 at a federal tax rate of 20.5 percent, according to income tax returns released by the White House today, three days prior to a scheduled Senate vote on the president’s call that millionaires be taxed at at least the same rate as middle class Americans. The Obamas reported an adjusted gross income of $789,674 in 2011, about half of which came from the president’s salary and the other half from sales proceeds of his books.

Mitt Romney accuses Obama of eroding constitutional rights

Mitt Romney, delivering a closely watched speech to the National Rifle Association, today accused President Obama of eroding gun owners’ rights but avoided any direct mention of controversial gun laws. Romney mentioned the word “gun” only once and did not address any current controversies surrounding the NRA, including its support for “stand-your-ground-laws,” which have been cited in the decision by police not to immediately arrest George Zimmerman in the shooting death of Trayvon Martin in Florida.

Senator John Kerry says much of world conflict fueled by clash of modernity with tribalism

Senator John Kerry, a possible secretary of state should President Obama win reelection, said today that much of the conflict around the globe stems from the clash between increasingly technological parts of the world and cultural tribalism embedded in largely uneducated and jobless populations. He said that while he’s proud of the charitable works of the United States and other countries, they need to recognize that many populations cannot quickly digest their societal norms as quickly as outsiders might like.

Joseph P. Kennedy III raised $1.3m in first three months of the year; Sean Bielat raised $175,000

Joseph P. Kennedy III, drawing heavily on his family’s national network of donors, raised $1.3 million in the first three months of the year to finance his bid to replace the retiring US Representative Barney Frank. Sean Bielat, Kennedy’s chief Republican opponent in the Fourth Congressional District, raised $175,000 in the first quarter, his campaign said this week.
