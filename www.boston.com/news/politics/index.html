Newt Gingrich appears on the verge of exit, reportedly will suspend his campaign next Tuesday

Newt Gingrich appears ready to concede the Republican nomination to Mitt Romney. After weeks of insisting it is premature to anoint Romney the GOP nominee, Gingrich sounded resigned to defeat Wednesday morning at a campaign stop in Cramerton, N.C. “I think you have to at some point be honest with what’s happening in the real world, as opposed to what you’d like to have happened,” Gingrich said a day after Romney swept primary contests in five states.

President Obama to hold first official campaign rallies on May 5

President Obama will officially kick off his campaign for re-election with rallies in two key swing states – Ohio and Virginia – on May 5, his campaign announced Wednesday. Not that Obama hasn’t been campaigning for months. Just this week, he conducted a whirlwind tour through the battleground states of North Carolina, Colorado, and Iowa to tout his college affordability plan, and to urge Congress not to double the interest rate on student loans. Technically, though, those were official trips.

Newt Gingrich’s delayed exit from GOP race underscores quixotic campaign

Reports that Newt Gingrich will end his presidential campaign - but not for a week - only underscore the quixotic nature of his candidacy. The former House speaker entered the race as the proverbial party big-thinker. But his notorious self-absorption raised the specter of a candidacy rooted not so much in winning his party’s nomination but salving a desire to reaffirm his stature within GOP circles. The suspected endgame? To reap rewards through future speaking fees and book sales.

Fox News ‘mole’ who leaked off-air footage of Mitt Romney says he’s being charged with grand larceny

A former Fox News Channel associate producer who provided off-air footage of Mitt Romney to Gawker said Wednesday he is being charged with grand larceny. Joe Muto, who wrote anonymously for Gawker as “The Fox Mole,” tweeted that he was served a search warrant at 6:30 a.m. Wednesday “by a very polite crew from the DA’s office” that “took my iPhone, laptop, some old notebooks.” The footage showed Romney discussing horseback riding with Sean Hannity before an interview.

Mitt Romney looks ahead as string of victories make clear rivals no longer a threat

Sweeping all five primaries Tuesday was almost an afterthought as Mitt Romney took the stage in New Hampshire to deliberately, if unofficially, put the Republican primary campaign behind him and shift his focus to his general election battle with President Obama. Instead, with a calmness borne by no longer having to glance back at carping rivals, the presumptive GOP nominee looked ahead to deliver not so much of a victory speech as his mass introduction to the American audience.
