Opinion: In DNC speech, Kerry continues war of words with Romney

When John Kerry was running for president, Mitt Romney was a harsh home-state critic. Now that Romney is running, Kerry is taking great delight in returning the, um, favor.

Bielat, Kennedy to vie for open House seat; Neal, Keating also defeat congressional challengers

The fight to fill Barney Frank’s House seat will pit Sean Bielat, a Republican who challenged Frank two years ago, against Joseph P. Kennedy III, a Democrat and scion of the famous political family, after both won party primaries on Thursday. The race was one of a handful of Congressional primaries across Massachusetts that set the field for the November election. In two contested Democratic primaries, Representatives William R. Keating of Bourne and Richard E. Neal of Springfield both defeated challengers.

Caroline Kennedy calls on women, young to rally for President Obama

Caroline Kennedy, joining a parade of others in her famed political family, addressed the Democratic National Convention on Thursday night and reaffirmed the influential endorsement she gave Barack Obama four years ago. “Back then, I was inspired by the promise of Barack Obama’s presidency,” she said. “Today, I’m inspired by his record.”

President Obama addresses delegates on last day of Democratic convention

Casting this year’s election as the clearest and most important choice in a generation, President Obama called on Americans to perservere through trying times toward a future where hard choices but shared sacrifice can create jobs as well as protect social services. “Over the next few years, big decisions will be made in Washington, on jobs and the economy; taxes and deficits; energy and education; war and peace – decisions that will have a huge impact on our lives and our children’s lives for decades to come,” Obama planned to say, according to advance excerpts of his speech.

John Kerry to praise President Obama’s conduct of foreign policy

Playing the role of both statesman and partisan provocateur, Massachusetts Senator John F. Kerry on Thursday evening is planning to make the case that President Obama “is giving new life and truth to America’s indispensable role in the world” and Republican challenger Mitt Romney is “out of touch at home, out of his depth abroad, and out of the mainstream,” according to excerpts of his prepared remarks. The chairman of the Senate Foreign Relations committee and his party’s nominee for president in 2004, Kerry was tapped to play chief surrogate for Obama on foreign policy at the Democrats convention.
