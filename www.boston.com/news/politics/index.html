Ad by pro-Obama super PAC exaggerates link between Mitt Romney and woman’s death from cancer

A highly sensitive ad by the pro-Obama super PAC Priorities USA Action overstates the connection between Mitt Romney’s business practices and the death of a steelworker’s wife, according to details reported by Politico and CNN. The ad features former GST Steel employee Joe Soptic describing his wife’s swift death from cancer after he lost his job and health insurance when the steel plant -- owned by by Bain Capital, the private equity firm Romney founded -- went bankrupt in 2001.

Scott Brown says effort to register welfare recipients geared to boost Elizabeth Warren

US Senator Scott Brown today criticized the state’s welfare department for sending voting registration forms to 478,000 people on public assistance, saying the mass mailing was a ploy to boost the ranks of Democratic voters and benefit rival Elizabeth Warren’s campaign. The state’s Department of Transitional Assistance last month sent registration forms as part of a settlement over a lawsuit alleging a violation of the federal “motor voter” law. It requires states to provide voter registration at various public offices.

Mitt Romney campaign says President Obama opposed work requirement in 1996 welfare reform law

Mitt Romney’s campaign fired a second volley Wednesday in the battle over welfare reform, painting President Obama as a proponent of government handouts who has long opposed placing work requirements on people who receive welfare benefits. The ad notes Obama’s opposition to the 1996 welfare reform law and accuses him of gutting welfare reform as president, repeating a charge the Romney campaign levied Tuesday in its first welfare ad.

Scott Brown, Elizabeth Warren start pouring their millions into Senate ads

Scott Brown and Elizabeth Warren are starting to spend the millions they have raised for their US Senate campaign. Both are out with new TV commercials today amid a quickening rotation of spots airing on Massachusetts stations. Brown, the Republican incumbent, is out with the third in a series of testimonials from Democrats who say they are backing him because of his bipartisanship. Warren’s ad continues a campaign outlining her governing priorities.

Pro-Obama super PAC ties Mitt Romney’s business practices to death of steelworker’s wife in new ad

A super PAC backing President Obama hit Mitt Romney Tuesday with a tough new ad that links the Republican challenger’s business practices to the death of a Kansas City steelworker’s wife. The ad by Priorities USA Action features Joe Soptic, a 21-year employee of GST Steel, who lost his job and the health insurance that came with it when the steel plant closed in 2001. The plant was owned by Bain Capital, the private equity firm founded by Romney.
