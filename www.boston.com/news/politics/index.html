President Obama asks ex-Harvard professor Ashton Carter to remain Pentagon’s No. 2

President Obama has personally asked Pentagon second-in-command and longtime Harvard professor Ashton Carter to continue running the day-to-day operations of armed forces during his second term, according to senior officials. Carter, who had been among the finalists to replace outgoing Defense Secretary Leon Panetta, will stay on as deputy secretary of defense to Chuck Hagel, the former senator awaiting confirmation for the top post. Carter is on leave from the Belfer Center at the Harvard Kennedy School.

President Obama nominating Jack Lew, former Tip O’Neill adviser, as next treasury secretary

President Obama today announced that he was nominating his chief of staff, Jacob L. Lew, to be his new treasury secretary, assigning one of the top cabinet roles to a longtime budget chief and former adviser to late House Speaker Tip O’Neill. Lew, who would replace Treasury Secretary Timothy Geithner, who is resigning, has served a number of roles in government over his three-decade career, including twice serving as director of the Office of Management and Budget.

John Kerry, incoming secretary of state, jokes that his own speeches are his cure for jet lag

As a former presidential candidate, globe-trotting lawmaker, and chairman of the Senate Foreign Relations Committee, Senator John Kerry already is a frequent flier. But nothing he’s done before will prepare him for the travel he will undertake if, as expected, he’s confirmed as secretary of state later this month. With that in mind, Kerry’s thoughts carry special weight as the Massachusetts Democrat suggests how he copes with jet lag, as he did in an light-hearted interview with Men’s Journal.

Progressive group launches petition urging appointment of Barney Frank as interim senator

he liberal advocacy group that helped draft Elizabeth Warren into the US Senate campaign in 2011 is now trying to make Barney Frank her junior senator. The Progressive Change Campaign Committee sent an email to 25,000 Massachusetts members Wednesday afternoon asking them to sign an online petition urging Governor Deval Patrick to appoint Frank as the state’s interim senator. The group also launched a website, AppointBarneyFrank.com.

Criticism, skepticism greet Patrick Kennedy’s effort to keep marijuana illegal

Former Rhode Island congressman Patrick J. Kennedy, a recovering addict, may seem an unlikely person to spearhead a new nationwide effort that wants to stymie the recent strides to legalize marijuana. But even before its Thursday launch, the new group has come under fire from legalization advocates who accuse Kennedy of being a hypocrite who wants to force all pot smokers into treatment.
