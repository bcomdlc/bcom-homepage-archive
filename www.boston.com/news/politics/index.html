Timothy Cahill indicted on charges of using official funds for campaign-boosting ads

A Suffolk County grand jury has handed up indictments against former state Treasurer Timothy P. Cahill on corruption charges after his campaign allegedly used $1.65 million in taxpayer-funded state lottery advertising to boost his floundering gubernatorial bid in 2010, a top state law enforcement official said today. Cahill was charged with directing the state Lottery to launch the media blitz that was designed to run up from mid-September though the November election. The indictment cites public corruption.

Senator Scott Brown and Plymouth DA Timothy Cruz call on Ecuador to return suspect in murder of mother and 2-year-old son

US Senator Scott Brown today called on Ecuador to return Luis Guaman to Massachusetts to stand trial on charges that he murdered Maria Avelina Palaguachi and her 2-year-old son, Brian. Brown appeared alongside Plymouth District Attorney Timothy J. Cruz in Brockton, where both men urged Secretary of State Hillary Rodham Clinton to intensify American diplomatic efforts with the South American country.

President Obama leads Mitt Romney by 9 points in swing states, poll shows

Women have propelled President Obama to a widening lead over Republican frontrunner Mitt Romney in a dozen swing states, according to a poll released Monday. The USA Today/Gallup Poll found Obama now enjoys a nine-point advantage over Romney, 51 percent to 42 percent. A month ago, the same poll showed the former Massachusetts governor with a two-point edge over the president. Much of Obama’s surge could be attributed to a widening gender gap. Women support Obama over Romney, 54 percent to 36 percent, the poll found. Romney’s narrow, 48-47 lead among men is within the poll’s margin of error.

Mitt Romney emerges as solitary figure within field seeking GOP nomination

As Mitt Romney delivered a speech Friday afternoon to kick off his Wisconsin campaign push following a week of fund-raising in California and Texas, he stood alone at the podium, surrounded by darkness, a solitary figure at center stage. It was emblematic of where Romney has come in his bid for the Republican presidential nomination, and the status he could solidify after Tuesday’s Wisconsin primary. One-by-one, Romney has outlasted his rivals, from Tim Pawlenty to Rick Perry.

Rep. Paul Ryan defends budget that would cut top tax rate to 25%

Wisconsin Representative Paul Ryan defended his budget proposal Sunday, saying it would establish a “better, simpler, more competitive tax system.” The House passed Ryan’s budget, 228-191, on Thursday, but the spending plan is expected to die in the Democrat-controlled Senate. The Republican budget would lower the top-level tax rate from 35 percent to 25 percent and thwart President Obama’s plan to raise taxes on couples earning more than $250,000 per year. Ryan argued the tax cuts would be offset by spending cuts and by the elimination of the “loopholes and tax shelters” decried by both parties.
