Rick Santorum stands by conservative record, calls Mitt Romney ‘liberal governor’

With just days to go before Tuesday’s Michigan primary, the GOP’s two leading candidates, Mitt Romney and Rick Santorum today appealed for support from Tea Party devotees and further attempted to burnish their conservative credentials. At an event in the working-class Detroit suburb of St. Clair Shores, Santorum mocked the former Massachusetts governor for questioning his conservative record. “It’s laughable for Mr. Romney to suggest that I am not conservative,” he said, and referred to his main rival for the Republican presidential nomination as “a liberal governor of Massachusetts.”

Romney criticized Santorum for not being conservative enough at Michigan forum

Mitt Romney amped up his criticism of Rick Santorum here this afternoon, giving a point-by-point critique of the former Pennsylvania senator’s record as the crucial Michigan primary enters its final days. Speaking at a Tea Party forum just hours after Santorum, Romney criticized his chief rival for not being sufficiently conservative. The lines of attack weren’t new – his campaign surrogates have levied similar charges – but Romney himself has generally not been as aggressive on the stump.

Mitt Romney focused on future, but past keeps coming up in Michigan

Mitt Romney often tries to focus his stump speech on his plans for the future, but whenever he’s in Michigan, it seems, he can’t get away from the past. “My dad and mom were in this very room,” he said tonight at Western Michigan University. “Hopefully my dad’s good luck running for governor in Michigan will rub off on me tonight.” He then recounted that, while driving across the state, his campaign bus drove past Brighton, Mich., where his parents are buried.

In economic speech in Detroit, Mitt Romney says his wife has two Cadillacs

Mitt Romney set out here to deliver a speech thick with policy, even warning his audience at the outset, “This is not exciting and barn-burning, but it’s important.” But it was a comment that he added as an aside that could again remind voters of his wealth and illustrate the struggles he can have when he deviates from talking points. He listed at least five cars that he and his wife have owned – and noted that she now drives a couple of Cadillacs, a type of car that could remind voters of the family’s wealth.

Governor Deval Patrick praises Barack Obama’s economic achievements

Governor Deval Patrick and his Democratic colleagues today lavished praise on President Obama and his economic record, following what Patrick characterized as a “productive and encouraging” meeting with the president and his economic team. “This is a president who saved the auto industry from extinction, the financial industry from self destruction and the economy from depression,” Patrick told a group of reporters gathered in front of the White House. The governors were in town for the National Governors Association winter meeting.
