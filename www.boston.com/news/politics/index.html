Representative Barney Frank accuses GOP of undermining Wall Street watchdog agencies

Representative Barney Frank, who coauthored the landmark bill overhauling Wall Street regulations, took aim at House Republicans on Friday for what he charged was an attempt to weaken the watchdog agencies that oversee the country’s financial markets. Frank, who serves as the ranking member on the House Financial Services Committee, is concerned that two spending bills released this week by House Republicans underfund two key Wall Street agencies: the Securities and Exchange Commission and the Commodity Futures Trading Commission.

New Mitt Romney commercial presents Mass. record in favorable light

Mitt Romney today harked back to his tenure as governor of Massachusetts in a new television commercial arguing that he will bring the same focus to his first day as president as he displayed during his leadership of the state. The presumptive Republican nominee boasts about his job-creation record, the balanced budgets he signed, and bipartisanship he fostered while serving as governor from 2003 to 2007. The commercial uses loose language that presents Romney’s record in the most favorable light.

Warren agrees to statewide consortium debate, Brown yet to commit

Elizabeth Warren, the Democratic US Senate candidate, announced this afternoon that she would accept a third debate with Scott Brown -- a forum sponsored by a consortium of media to be televised on multiple outlets throughout the state. But Brown did not commit. The two candidates have mutually agreed to two televised debates, one hosted by WBZ-TV reporter Jon Keller and another hosted by a consortium of Western Massachusetts media.

Barack Obama campaign questions Mitt Romney’s secrecy over his personal finances

The Obama campaign raised questions Thursday over Mitt Romney’s secrecy when it comes to his finances, especially with regards to his investments and whether he pays his fair share of taxes, and called upon the Republican candidate to release his retirement contract from Bain Capital. In a conference call with reporters, Robert Bauer, general counsel for the Obama campaign, suggested that Romney may still be working for Bain in some capacity despite Romney’s claim to have left the company in 1999.

Teachers union strikes deal with education reform group to curb seniority rights

The state’s largest teachers union has struck a deal in which it would give up significant seniority rights for members, in exchange for a commitment from an education reform group to withdraw a far more sweeping proposed ballot initiative. Paul Toner, the president of the Massachusetts Teachers Association, confirmed today his group has been meeting with Beacon Hill legislative leaders to brief them on the deal and present them with a draft bill that would curb the influence of seniority in the placement and firing of teaches.
