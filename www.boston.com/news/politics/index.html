Kerry announces $60m in Syria aid

Secretary of State John Kerry announced here today that the United States is preparing to provide $60 million to the Syrian opposition in an effort to help strengthen those trying to oust Syrian President Bashir al-Assad. The funds will not be used to arm the fighters, which would have been a more aggressive step that some had hoped the US would take to convince Assad to step down. Instead, the US financial support will mostly be used to allow Syrian opposition leaders to provide security, sanitation, and educational services in areas that they now control.

Five candidates submit required number of signatures for special election for US Senate

All five candidates in the special election for US Senate have collected well above the required amount of certified signatures to qualify for their party’s primary ballots, according to the initial tally at the 5 p.m. Wednesday deadline. According to the Central Voter Registry at the secretary of state’s office, Democratic US Representative Edward M. Markey led the field by day’s end, with 33,799, followed by his rival for the party nomination, US Representative Stephen P. Lynch, who amassed 25,104.

Scott Brown to hold Chris Christie fund-raiser Friday

Former Senator Scott Brown and a group of top Massachusetts Republicans are planning to hold a high-dollar fundraiser for New Jersey Governor Chris Christie on Friday night. The fundraiser, which asks for maximum $3,800 contributions, is being held at the Boston home of Christopher and Jean Egan. The fundraiser is for Christie’s 2013 reelection campaign for governor, but it could also provide some early indications of support should he decide to run for president in 2016.

Secretary of State John Kerry revisits childhood days in Berlin visit

John Kerry has done town hall meetings in Manchester, N.H. He’s fielded questions about corn subsidies from voters in Des Moines, and stumped in cities across Massachusetts. But on Tuesday, the new secretary of state brought those political skills to Germany, holding a town hall meeting here with students in the foreign country where Kerry himself spent time as a child. He cracked jokes, often at his own expense. At times, he evaded the question asked and gave the answer he preferred instead.

Mass. congressional delegation says sequester cuts would hurt children, the elderly, and the poor

Members of the Massachusetts congressional delegation spoke out passionately this morning against upcoming automatic federal spending cuts. Speaking at a rally at an anti-poverty agency in downtown Boston, they argued that the the impending cuts shouldn’t be seen in terms of numbers but in terms of the effect they would have on the lives of children, the elderly, and the poor.
