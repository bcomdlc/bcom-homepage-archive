Deval Patrick: Striking down Obama health law would be ‘a tragedy’

Governor Deval Patrick said this morning that Massachusetts residents will probably be unaffected, but millions of others across the country could lose their health insurance, if the Supreme Court strikes down President Obama’s health care law. “It is unlikely that if they go in what I view as the wrong direction, and strike down all or some of the bill, that it will affect what we’re doing here in Massachusetts, because that’s based on state law,” Patrick said during his monthly appearance on WTKK-FM. “But it would be a tragedy, because we’re a country, and the issue of the cost and access to health care is a national issue.”

Scott Brown, Elizabeth Warren debate across media spectrums, political universes

The image of America as a bifurcated political landscape was brought into full relief Wednesday night, as Scott Brown and Elizabeth Warren debated each other across media spectrums and before hand-picked partisan hosts. The first candidate confrontation in what may be the most hard fought US Senate race in the country was conducted not face to face, but at arm’s length. Brown’s chosen venue was WBZ-AM and conservative talk show host Dan Rea. Warren’s was MSNBC and liberal Rachel Maddow.

Scott Brown to appear in radio debate with no opponent amid posturing with Elizabeth Warren

What would happen if someone held a debate and only one candidate showed up? Massachusetts residents will find out tonight, when Senator Scott Brown appears on WBZ-AM for a debate Elizabeth Warren rejected after the Republican incumbent rejected a televised debate she had accepted. The posturing will result in Brown having an hourlong conversation with conservative talk show host Dan Rea, while Warren readies to appear immediately afterward with liberal Rachel Maddow on her MSNBC television program.

Scott Brown will attend Republican National Convention, but with no speaking role

Senator Scott Brown is planning to attend the Republican National Convention this August but will not have a speaking role as his party gathers to tap his fellow Massachusetts Republican as its presidential nominee. Of any Republican in the country, perhaps no candidate has more to risk by being at the convention than Brown, who is running for reelection as an independent-minded Republican in a deep blue state. Also, Mitt Romney doesn’t fare well in public opinion polls in his home state.

Mitt Romney campaigns in Virginia as new poll shows him with a small lead

Mitt Romney campaigns Wednesday in the crucial swing state of Virginia, where a new poll gives the Republican presidential candidate a 5-point advantage over President Obama. The automated poll of 1,100 likely Virginia voters by We Ask America said Romney had 48 percent support, to Obama’s 43.3 percent. The margin of error is nearly 3 percentage points. Romney’s schedule takes him late in the afternoon to EIT LLC, an electronics company in Sterling, Va., a Washington exurb in the northern part of the state.
