GOP US Senate candidate Michael Sullivan says he’d vote against gun background checks

Republican US Senate candidate Michael J. Sullivan, who previously held the nation’s top post regulating firearms, said in a WFXT-TV (Channel 25) debate tonight that he would vote against the Senate’s current measure that aims to close the gun show loophole because there is no such exception.

Joseph Kennedy III creates leadership PAC

U.S. Representative Joseph Kennedy III, a freshman Democrat from Massachusetts, has launched a leadership PAC called “4MAPAC,” a signal of his intention to use the Kennedy name to build a political base through fundraising. Kennedy registered the Political Action Committee with the Federal Election Commission on Tuesday. A Kennedy aide said the PAC will allow Kennedy to raise higher amounts of money and contribute to the races of fellow members and others moving forward, particularly critical as Democrats strategize to win back the House in 2014.

Tea Partiers to hold telethon for Mass. GOP Senate candidate — live from Harrah’s in Reno

A Tea Party-affiliated political action committee that caused a stir in the US Senate race last week is now planning a weekend telethon to benefit Republican candidate Michael J. Sullivan — live from Reno. “The telethon will broadcast LIVE from Harrah’s Reno hotel, but we are NEVER, EVER to mention that we will be doing the telethon from Reno,” said an organizer’s e-mail, obtained by the Globe. “We want viewers to assume the broadcast will be taking place from Massachusetts.” The e-mail was circulated by Joseph Wierzbicki, a Tea Party leader and executive director of the Conservative Campaign Committee, which has been trying to rally conservatives across the nation to raise money for Sullivan. The group hopes to raise $50,000 during Sunday’s eight-hour telethon.

Sullivan attacks Gomez in new TV ad

US Senate hopeful Michael J. Sullivan went on the attack against fellow Republican candidate Gabriel E. Gomez, tying Gomez to President Obama in a new a television ad released today. The ad uses a letter Gomez wrote to Gov. Deval Patrick in January, putting his name forward to be appointed as interim Senator for the seat formerly held by John Kerry. “Gabriel Gomez wrote to Deval Patrick saying he ‘supported President Obama’,” a female narrator says in the 30 second spot, Sullivan’s first TV ad.

Hours after being criticized by Senate leader, Governor Deval Patrick signals agreement possible

Governor Deval Patrick said today he is open to the state Senate’s transportation financing plan, comments that could signal the beginning of the end of a tense dispute between the governor and legislative leaders over taxes and transit funding. The governor said the $805 million Senate bill could be a middle ground between his $1 billion tax increase for transportation and the $500 million tax hike that the House passed earlier this week.
