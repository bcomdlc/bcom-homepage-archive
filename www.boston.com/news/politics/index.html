Rep. Paul Ryan defends budget that would cut top tax rate to 25%

Wisconsin Representative Paul Ryan defended his budget proposal Sunday, saying it would establish a “better, simpler, more competitive tax system.” The House passed Ryan’s budget, 228-191, on Thursday, but the spending plan is expected to die in the Democrat-controlled Senate. The Republican budget would lower the top-level tax rate from 35 percent to 25 percent and thwart President Obama’s plan to raise taxes on couples earning more than $250,000 per year. Ryan argued the tax cuts would be offset by spending cuts and by the elimination of the “loopholes and tax shelters” decried by both parties.

Newt Gingrich pledges to back Mitt Romney, if Romney wins GOP nomination

Republican presidential candidate Newt Gingrich insisted Sunday that he is “not going to concede” the GOP nomination to frontrunner Mitt Romney, but said he would back Romney strongly in a general election contest against President Obama. Gingrich’s talk of supporting a current rival was a departure from his consistently defiant campaign rhetoric. The former House speaker has often decried people to whom he refers broadly as “the elites” for prematurely signaling the end of his campaign.

Vice President Joe Biden says Mitt Romney lacks knowledge about foreign policy

Vice President Joe Biden on Sunday became the latest to blast Republican presidential frontrunner Mitt Romney for his view of Russia, saying the former Massachusetts governor is unknowledgeable about foreign policy. The foreign policy critique was part of a wholesale condemnation of Romney’s worldview by the vice president. Biden levied the familiar charge that Romney is “out of touch” with Average Americans.

Rick Santorum says he’s still a viable presidential candidate

Rick Santorum strongly rejected calls for him to drop out of the Republican nominating race, and disputed the notion that a contested convention would be disastrous for the party. “There is one thing worse than a convention fight, and that’s picking the wrong candidate,” Santorum said Sunday on NBC’s “Meet the Press.”

US Rep. Lynch gets another parking pass; congressional vehicle’s permit appears to have expired years ago

US Representative Stephen Lynch has apparently been getting a pass on parking in his hometown of South Boston for years. His second vehicle -- this one a personal car with an official congressional license plate -- bears a residential parking sticker that expired six years ago. The sedan, marked by the license plate “US Congress 9” and two Lynch campaign stickers in the rear window, also bears a South Boston residential parking sticker that expired Dec. 31, 2006.
