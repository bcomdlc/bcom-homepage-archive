Mitt Romney says he must battle a ‘vast left-wing conspiracy’ in the media

Mitt Romney on Tuesday cast himself as the target of a “vast left-wing conspiracy” in the press, saying “many in the media are inclined to do the president’s bidding.” Romney’s comments conjured statements made 14 years ago by Hillary Clinton, who said a “vast right-wing conspiracy” was responsible for accusations of sexual misconduct against then-President Bill Clinton. Romney has said he cannot expect fairness from most outlets but that Fox News “is watched by the true believers.”

House Speaker John Boehner and Senator Mitch McConnell throw support behind Mitt Romney

A week after Rick Santorum abandoned his bid for the Republican presidential nomination, House Speaker John Boehner and Senate Minority Leader Mitch McConnell on Tuesday offered belated endorsements to Mitt Romney, now the presumptive GOP nominee. Boehner will chair the Republican National Convention in August and had refused to back any candidate during a testy primary season. But he said Tuesday that he “will be proud to support Mitt Romney.” McConnell hinted earlier this month that he would support Romney.

Pro-Obama super PAC spending $660,000 on TV ad attacking Mitt Romney in swing states

Priorities USA Action, a super PAC founded by a pair of former aides to President Obama, will spend $660,000 to air a television ad attacking Mitt Romney in four swing states. The expense is by far the largest for Priorities USA, and the ad accuses Romney, the presumptive Republican nominee, of plotting to slash social programs while cutting taxes for wealthy Americans, like himself. It will run in Ohio, Florida, Iowa and Virginia, expected battleground states in November’s general election.

Mitt Romney to give a ‘prebuttal’ to President Obama at site of Democratic convention

Mitt Romney on Wednesday plans to travel near the site of this summer’s Democratic National Convention, aiming to give a “prebuttal” to President Obama’s nomination acceptance speech in Charlotte – nearly five months before it is scheduled to occur. The presumptive Republican presidential nominee is planning to deliver the speech at A Roof with a View, a reception area that happens to be across the street from the Bank of America Stadium, where Obama will give his acceptance speech in September.

Scott Brown, Elizabeth Warren may release tax returns at Globe’s request

Elizabeth Warren’s US Senate campaign said today that the Democrat would release two years of her personal tax returns on the condition that Senator Scott Brown, the Republican incumbent, does the same. The Brown campaign responded that it may go further, releasing six years, but has not yet committed. The Globe asked both campaigns this morning to release six years worth of returns, including the filing due today. Their Senate race is one of the most highly watched in the country.
