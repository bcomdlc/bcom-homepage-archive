Michael Jordan to headline fundraising game for President Obama

In 1990, basketball superstar Michael Jordan famously refused to endorse Democrat Harvey Gantt in a North Carolina Senate race, saying “Republicans buy shoes, too.” Twenty-two years later, Jordan is apparently no longer worried about sneaker sales. The Hall of Famer will headline a celebrity basketball game that will double as a fund-raiser for President Obama, the Obama campaign announced Tuesday. The basketball game is being used as a lure for donors.

Elizabeth Warren backs off demand that Scott Brown release more tax returns

Elizabeth Warren has demanded that Senator Scott Brown release more years of his tax returns. The only problem was that Brown, her Republican rival, has already released six years of tax returns while Warren has refused to release more than four years of her filings. Asked to reconcile that apparent conflict, Warren backed off her demand hours later, saying six years was enough. She did not, however, offer to release any more of her IRS filings.

Group opposing Boy Scouts’ ban on gay members counts Mitt Romney as a supporter

Mitt Romney is sticking to his position that gay people should be allowed to join the Boy Scouts of America. Romney first declared his support for gay scouts and leaders in 1994, during an unsuccessful run for US Senate. In a debate that year, Romney said, “I feel that all people should be able to participate in the Boy Scouts regardless of their sexual orientation.” A Romney spokeswoman told the Associated Press that Romney feels the same way today.

Rick Santorum lands primetime speaking role at Republican National Convention

Rick Santorum, the former Pennsylvania senator who provided Mitt Romney’s stiffest test during the GOP primary season, will deliver a primetime address to the Republican National Convention later this month. Republican National Committee Chairman Reince Priebus on Tuesday announced speaking roles for Santorum, former Florida Governor Jeb Bush, Kentucky Senator Rand Paul and Oklahoma Governor Mary Fallin.

Governor Deval Patrick signs new health care cost measure; bill builds on law passed when GOP candidate Mitt Romney was governor

Governor Deval Patrick, in a State House ceremony packed with hospital executives, healthcare advocates, and lawmakers, signed a sweeping bill today that seeks to rein in the state’s spiraling health costs. The first-in-the nation legislation is considered the second phase of the groundbreaking universal healthcare law that was signed by former governor Mitt Romney in 2006.
