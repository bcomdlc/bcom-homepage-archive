Former Senator Scott Brown in negotiations to appear on Fox News

Former US Senator Scott Brown is in negotiations with Fox News to appear on the network, a spokesperson for the national network told the Globe Wednesday afternoon. Brown, who lost his reelection bid in November, stunned the Massachusetts political world on Friday when he announced he would not run for the US Senate seat opened by John F. Kerry’s departure to become Secretary of State. It was unclear what role Brown might have on the network.

Mary Beth Heffernan confirmed as district court judge on a 7-1 vote by Governor’s Council

Mary Beth Heffernan, Governor Deval Patrick’s former public safety chief, today was confirmed on a 7-1 vote by the Governor’s Council as a district court judge. Councilors, who held a hearing on Heffernan’s appointment last week, offered few comments before the quick approval, and Heffernan herself was not present.

State Rep. Dan Winslow ‘99 percent’ sure he will run for US Senate

State Representative Daniel B. Winslow, a Norfolk Republican, formed an exploratory committee Tuesday and said he is “99 percent” sure that he will run for US Senate in the special election to replace John F. Kerry. Many of the more well-known figures in the party, including former senator Scott Brown and former lieutenant governor Kerry Healey, have taken themselves out of consideration.

US Department of Justice to brief House committee on Aaron Swartz prosecution

The Department of Justice agreed yesterday to brief a congressional committee on US Attorney Carmen Ortiz’s prosecution of Aaron Swartz, the 26-year-old Internet activist who committed suicide last month after battling federal hacking charges for two years, officials said.
