Deval Patrick’s Obama friendship, Romney perspective, create convention demand

He may be governor of Massachusetts, but Deval Patrick will be a man about town in Charlotte this week. The twin virtues of his close connection to President Obama and leadership of the state previously governed by Republican presidential nominee Mitt Romney make Patrick a hot commodity at the 2012 Democratic National Convention. It all starts at 8 a.m., when he addresses delegates from the battleground state of Virginia. Then he attends a breakfast with his homestate delegation.

President Obama, Mitt Romney tied heading into Democratic convention, poll shows

President Obama will enter this week’s Democratic National Convention in a tie with Mitt Romney, after the Republican challenger got a slight boost from his own convention last week, according to a Reuters/Ipsos poll published Sunday. The presidential race is deadlocked, the survey showed, with both candidates enjoying 45-percent support among likely voters nationwide. In a survey taken a week ago, before the Republican National Convention in Tampa, Romney trailed Obama by 4 points, 46 percent to 42 percent.

Private equity firm founded by Mitt Romney under investigation for tax strategy

New York’s attorney general is investigating whether executives at Bain Capital, the private equity firm founded by Republican presidential nominee Mitt Romney, improperly avoided paying $200 million in taxes, according to a report by the New York Times. The attorney general, Eric T. Schneiderman, has subpoenaed documents that would show whether some fund management fees were converted to fund investments. Profits on investments are taxed at a much lower rate than management fees, which count as earned income.

Mitt Romney’s assertions don’t always ring true, according to fact checkers

Mitt Romney kept fact-checkers busy Thursday night, as claims and assertions in his acceptance speech come under scrutiny. On the morning after, some contentions made by the man who would be president appear as empty as the chair in Clint Eastwood’s schtick. Romney was off base on charging that President Obama began his term with an apology tour. And Romney’s recollection of how Bain Capital began seemed like history rewritten.

Karl Rove screens Scott Brown ad in Tampa, later sees senator

Hours before Karl Rove saw Senator Scott Brown at the Republican National Convention in Tampa on Thursday, he screened an advertisement that related to the Massachusetts Senate race, according to BusinessWeek. But a spokesman for Rove’s super PAC, American Crossroads, said the ad was one of two that ran in Massachusetts in November and December. That was before Brown and his opponent, Democrat Elizabeth Warren, signed a pledge intended to keep outside groups from running commercials designed to influence their race.
