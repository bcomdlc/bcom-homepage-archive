Wisconsin Congressman Paul Ryan endorses Mitt Romney for president

Wisconsin Congressman Paul Ryan announced his endorsement of Mitt Romney for the Republican presidential nomination on Fox News Friday, four days ahead of the Wisconsin primary and a week after Romney threw his support behind the House budget committee chairman’s budget blueprint. “Who will make the best president? And who has the best chance of defeating Barack Obama?” Ryan said. “In my opinion, Mitt Romney is clearly that person.”

Mitt Romney’s Wisconsin campaign swing takes him to ‘Harvard of the Midwest’

Mitt Romney’s campaign swing through Wisconsin today takes him to Lawrence University. It was founded by a philanthropist with connections to Lawrence, Mass. It was once headed a president who went on to run Harvard University, the same campus where Romney received his business and law degrees. And among its better-known graduates are Liz Brunner of WCVB-TV, Terry Moran of “Nightline,” and former US ambassador to India David Mulford. Appleton is also where Senator Joseph R. McCarthy is buried.

Senate Republicans defeat White House proposal to strip subsidies from oil companies

The Senate today rejected a White House plan that would strip $24 billion in subsidies to the country’s largest oil companies, which could become a potent election-year issue among voters disgruntled by escalating gas prices. The 51-47 vote was mostly along party lines, with the Bay State’s senators split accordingly. Most Democrats, including Senator John Kerry, voted in favor of eliminating the subsidies but fell far short of the 60 votes needed to withstand a filibuster. Senator Scott Brown joined most Republicans in rejecting the effort.

Scott Brown’s campaign says it will pay $35,000 to comply with ‘People’s Pledge’ violation

Scott Brown’s campaign plans to announce today that it will write a $34,545 check to the charity of Elizabeth Warren’s choice, after both sides agreed that a third-party group had broken the so-called “People’s Pledge” to keep interest groups from influencing their US Senate election. Earlier this week, the American Petroleum Institute took out radio and print ads denouncing the Democrats’ plan to eliminate subsidies for energy companies. The industry-funded group urged Brown to oppose the measure.

Mitt Romney, Newt Gingrich reported to have had secret meeting in New Orleans last week

By all public appearances, the three men seem to want little to do with one another. But in private, three of the remaining four Republican presidential contenders communicate often, according to a spokesman for one of the campaigns. “Gingrich, Santorum, and Romney speak frequently,” R.C. Hammond, a spokesman for Newt Gingrich, said in an email. The subject has received new attention amid reports that Gingrich and Mitt Romney met secretly in New Orleans on Friday.
