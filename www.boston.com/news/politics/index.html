Most Americans want critics of health care law to move onto more pressing national concerns, Kaiser poll finds

A new poll released by Kaiser Family Foundation on Monday found that following last week’s Supreme Court decision upholding President Obama’s controversial health care overhaul, a majority of Americans now say they would like to see the law’s critics stop their efforts to block the law’s implementation and move onto other national problems.

Embattled bank executive no longer co-hosting London fund-raiser for Mitt Romney

Mitt Romney’s campaign says that Bob Diamond, the embattled chief executive of Barclays bank, will no longer be co-hosting a London fund-raiser for the presumptive Republican nominee. Diamond, a Concord, Mass., native who oversees one of the world’s largest banks, has been embroiled in a rate-fixing scandal in Britain that is threatening to expand.

Mitt Romney plans trip to Israel this summer

Mitt Romney is planning a trip to Israel this summer to meet with Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, according to a campaign official, in a trip abroad that could work to bolster Romney’s foreign policy credentials. The former Massachusetts governor is planning to meet with a variety of officials while on the trip, including Palestinian Authority President Salam Fayyad; President Shimon Peres of Israel; and American Ambassador Daniel B. Shapiro.

Barney Frank keeping wedding details under wraps (pssst -- it’s on a holiday weekend in July)

It’s not exactly the secret celebrity wedding of the season, but it still might be high-wattage enough -- at least in political circles -- to attract unwanted paparazzi. So Representative Barney Frank is attempting to keep the timing of his marriage to Jim Ready under wraps. It’s not working too well. Frank himself has leaked enough clues that it is easy to conclude the wedding is this Saturday afternoon, in a private ceremony in Newton.

In awkward agreement, Mitt Romney backs President Obama’s contention that health care mandate is not a tax

Mitt Romney’s campaign added one more spin Monday to the dizzying debate over what to call money owed to the government by people who flout the national health care law’s individual insurance mandate. Romney spokesman Eric Fehrnstrom said the presumptive Republican presidential nominee agrees with the Obama administration’s insistence that the “shared responsibility payment,” as it is called in the Affordable Care Act, should be described as a penalty, or a fee, or a fine -- but not a tax.
