Super Tuesday awaits the GOP

Next Tuesday, voters in 10 states head to the polls. Republican candidates need 1,144 delegates to receive the presidential nomination, and 437 delegates will be awarded from the primaries.

Mitt Romney has chance to make headway Super Tuesday despite campaign’s adversities

Two things seem true as Mitt Romney approaches the Super Tuesday vote. First, this campaign has aged him like nothing else during the nearly 65 years in his life. Second, the process has to be the most humiliating of that life. Yet Romney has a chance to win enough primaries and caucuses not to seal the nomination but make it highly unlikely that remaining rivals Rick Santorum, Newt Gingrich, or Ron Paul can do so themselves the rest of the way.

Mitt Romney gets endorsements from Eric Cantor, Tom Coburn

House Majority Leader Eric Cantor of Virginia and Oklahoma Senator Tom Coburn today endorsed Mitt Romney in the Republican presidential race. Both state hold primaries Tuesday.

Critics call for Mitt Romney to issue a stronger repudiation of Rush Limbaugh’s attack on Georgetown student

Mitt Romney was criticized this morning for not more forcefully denouncing Rush Limbaugh’s comments that a Georgetown law student was a “slut” for advocating for expanded birth control coverage.

Newt Gingrich primary mission: win the South

Speaking today on ABC’s This Week, Former House Speaker Newt Gingrich said he believes he will win Georgia, the state he represented in Congress and one of 10 states voting on Tuesday. He also predicted that he will do well in other southern states.

Rick Santorum acknowledges he may have misstated Barack Obama’s position on education and training

Republican presidential candidate Rick Santorum acknowledged today that he may have misstated President Obama’s position on education when he called Obama a snob for saying he “wants everyone in America to go to college.”
