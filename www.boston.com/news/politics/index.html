White House honors Boston-based programs as ‘Champions of Change’

The White House honored Emmett Folgert, founder and executive director of the Dorchester Youth Collaborative, on Wednesday as one of a dozen leaders named “Champions of Change” for their work as part of a new national effort to prevent youth violence. On Thursday the White House will recognize the Chinese Immigrant Student Leadership program based out of Boston’s Charlestown High School for the pilot program’s work in inspiring the Asian American community and giving the underserved a voice.

Mitt Romney rebukes President Obama for hiding his real views from public

Mitt Romney, hours after a triumphant victory in three states, on Wednesday delivered a stinging rebuke of President Obama, saying he was playing “hide and seek” and withholding his real views from the American public. “He wants us to re-elect him so we can find out what he will actually do,” Romney told a group of media executives in Washington. “With all the challenges the nation faces, this is not the time for President Obama’s hide-and-seek campaign.”

President Obama signs insider trading bill championed by Senator Scott Brown

President Obama today signed into law a congressional insider trading bill championed by Senator Scott Brown of Massachusetts, a Republican, and hailed the legislation as an example of bipartisanship in the nation’s capital. Brown seemed to relish the moment, smiling broadly as the president signed the bill, which the Bay State senator considers a signature accomplishment.

Federal judge orders Justice Department to acknowledge courts’ power, after President Obama says Supreme Court should not overturn health care law

A federal judge in Texas has demanded the Department of Justice acknowledge courts’ authority to strike down acts of Congress, after President Obama said Monday that a Supreme Court ruling against his signature health care law would be an “unprecedented” display of “judicial activism.”

Addressing editors in Washington, Mitt Romney becomes media critic

Mitt Romney is often guarded around members of the media. As governor, he famously installed velvet ropes at the State House that prevented much interaction with reporters. During this campaign, he’s gone weeks at times without holding a press conference. But on Wednesday morning, before a ballroom of media executives, Romney played the role of media critic. He lamented some of the cuts in the news industry, and some shifts that have come with a 24-hour news cycle.
