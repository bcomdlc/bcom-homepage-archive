Clinton rouses Democrats with arguments for Obama

Striving to build momentum and stoke enthusiasm for President Obama’s upcoming acceptance speech, Democrats on Wednesday rolled out one of their biggest stars and one of their newest ones as they redoubled their assault on Republican nominee Mitt Romney. In the second day of the Democratic National Convention, former President Bill Clinton and Senate candidate Elizabeth Warren planned to urge the country to stay the course.

Elizabeth Warren aims to boost the president nationally and herself at home

Elizabeth Warren used a national audience Wednesday night to deliver a campaign message that has hit a roadblock back at home. The US Senate candidate tried to boost President Obama’s reelection prospects at the Democratic National Convention by talking about his commitment to her pet cause: creating a consumer watchdog agency, most especially in the aftermath of the 2008 economic collapse. In doing so, Warren tried to stave off ticket-splitting voters who may back Republican Senator Scott Brown.

Workers of Bain Capital-funded companies complain of treatment to Democratic convention

Three former employees of Bain Capital-funded companies who lost their jobs urged delegates to support President Obama at the convention Wednesday night. They were familiar names and faces to close watchers of the campaign, two of them union officials who have appeared in numerous news stories and sometimes campaign ads criticizing Bain and its founder, Mitt Romney.

Elizabeth Warren makes case for President Obama

Elizabeth Warren planned to take the primetime stage for her first Democratic convention to deliver a populist attack against Wall Street, in a speech intended to put the Democratic Party on the side of the middle class, according to an advance copy of her remarks Wednesday night. “People feel like the system is rigged against them,” she planned to tell delegates at the Democratic National Convention. “And here’s the painful part: They’re right.”

Democrats release portions of Elizabeth Warren’s speech ahead of tonight’s delivery

The Democratic National Committee released portions of Elizabeth Warren’s speech, as prepared for delivery after 10 p.m. tonight. Warren, a Democrat in a tough campaign against Senator Scott Brown, is hoping to use the speech to bolster her campaign as she promotes President Obama’s reelection campaign. Warren is scheduled to speak shortly before Bill Clinton, the evening’s headliner, who will be introduced by Los Angeles Mayor Antonio R. Villaraigosa.
