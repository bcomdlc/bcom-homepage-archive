Rudy Giuliani’s endorsement of Mitt Romney breathtaking because of past - and present

Rudy Giuliani endorsed Mitt Romney for president on Monday, a reversal of this 2008 campaign against the former Massachusetts governor. But his comments can’t be viewed just against the context of what he said four years ago; time and circumstances change of course, and Romney has to be compared to the candidates running now, not then. They are breathtaking, though, when considering the pointed criticism Giuliani leveled at Romney not in 2008 but just this past December, January, and February.

Mitt Romney, Scott Brown join President Obama’s call to stop student loan interest rates from doubling

President Obama on Monday threw more weight into his push to prevent federal student loan interest rates from doubling and found an unexpected ally working alongside him: Mitt Romney. In a pair of conference calls with reporters surrogates set out to contrast the president’s effort to freeze Stafford loan interest rates at 3.4 percent with Romney’s alleged indifference. But Romney said at a campaign stop in Philadelphia that he, too, wants to keep interest rates where they are.

Pro-Romney super PAC declines to give details behind $400,000 donation

The super PAC promoting Mitt Romney’s presidential candidacy would not reveal precisely Monday who wrote a $400,000 check to the group in March. Restore Our Future said, however, who did not: Scott DeSano, the man whose business address is listed on a report to the Federal Election Commission. DeSano said he has not contributed any money to Restore Our Future, and a spokeswoman for the super PAC told the Globe that a clerical error was responsible for the incorrect attribution.

John Kerry pays tribute to former Kennedy aide who pushed for health care law

Senator John F. Kerry today paid tribute to Stacey Sachs, an aide to the late Senator Edward M. Kennedy who died over the weekend after helping achieve her former boss’s goal of a federal universal health care law. She succumbed to cancer on Saturday in Bethesda, Md., at age 50. “For many of us, Stacey was a steady and unchanging sight in this ever-changing institution,” Kerry said in a statement introduced to the US Senate record.

Elizabeth Warren releases second TV ad of US Senate campaign

Elizabeth Warren today released the second ad of her US Senate campaign, a 30-spot that highlights her middle-class childhood while also adding a new prong to her anti-Wall Street message. “Today, Washington lets big corporations like GE pay nothing - zero - in taxes, while kids are left drowning in debt to get an education,” Warren says. “This isn’t about economics; it’s about our values.” The interest rates on Stafford loans may double on July 1.
